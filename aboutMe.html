My Story

  I always wanted to be an engineer. Throughout high school and college, I tried my absolute hardest to make my dream come true. I was doing very well, even in my advanced engineering classes. However, in these classes, I always felt like something was missing.
  It took me three years to finally figure out what that feeling was. In my Thermodynamics II final, we had to construct an ideal diesel and gas engine in Matlab. I had some programming experience before this project in CS150, but I had forgotten most of the basics. I had an A in the class and the final was worth 30% of the grade. I knew I had to learn as much programming as possible to keep my grade. I struggled and struggled and spent probably 50 hours on a really basic project, but I did it, I got the A.
  I was always interested in computer science, and although my project was really basic it gave me the confidence to finally make the jump into programming. After looking into all the technologies and exciting things going on in the computer world, that missing feeling I had about engineering was gone. Although I enjoyed engineering, coding felt like I was at the cutting edge of technology, with an amazingly supportive community. This community of ambitious people and ever-changing technologies makes me excited to work and learn new things. Ever since that project, I have been studying web development, and I plan on always learning and improving my skills for the future.
